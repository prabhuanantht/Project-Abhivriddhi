“Teaching is the profession that teaches all the other professions ”

About

The primary role of a teacher is to deliver classroom instruction to help students learn. A teacher is someone who provides assistance in learning life as well as educational skills. In a career as teacher, he or she plans a curriculum to provide lessons in the class depending on the student’s grade level. A teacher is a professional who is less likely to lose his or her temper with students. He or she keeps patience in teaching students and answers each student’s questions until their doubts are not cleared.

Roles and Responsibilities

The roles and responsibilities of a teacher can vary depending on the level of education (e.g., preschool, elementary, middle, high school, or higher education) and the specific subject they teach. However, there are some fundamental responsibilities that are common to most teaching positions. Here are the key roles and responsibilities of a teacher:

  • Designing lesson plans and learning activities that align with educational standards and cater to diverse learning needs.
  • Establishing a positive and inclusive classroom environment where students feel safe, respected, and motivated to learn.
  • Creating fair and valid assessments to measure students' understanding of the material.
  • Identifying students who may require additional support, such as those with learning disabilities or language barriers.
  • Engaging in continuous professional development to stay updated on educational best practices and advancements in the field.

Eligibility

Here are some basic qualifications you need to have to become a Teacher in India:

  • Completed your secondary education up to the 12th standard, obtaining the necessary grades that is required in your reagion.
  • To become a certified teacher, a Bachelor's in Education (B.Ed.) degree is required.
  • Some countries or states require aspiring teachers to obtain a teaching certification or license after completing their education.This often involves passing exams that assess pedagogical knowledge and teaching skills.

Teaching Courses

Aspirants can select and pursue the degrees to build a career in the domain of Teaching . The courses are as follows:

  • Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.):
    B.Ed. is a popular undergraduate teaching course designed to train candidates as educators. It is typically a two-year program and is required to become a certified teacher in schools.

  • Diploma in Education (D.Ed.) / Elementary Teacher Training (ETT):
    D.Ed. or ETT courses focus on preparing teachers specifically for primary and elementary level education.

  • Integrated B.A. B.Ed. / B.Sc. B.Ed:
    Some universities offer integrated programs that combine a bachelor's degree with a teaching degree, enabling students to pursue their subject of interest along with teacher training.

  • Master of Education (M.Ed.):
    M.Ed. is a postgraduate course that provides advanced training in educational theory, research, and curriculum development.
